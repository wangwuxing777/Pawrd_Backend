## Benefits

### Section I - Surgical Benefit
> Source: p2
> Clause: Section I
> Unit: benefit

#### 1. Clinical and Surgical Benefit
> Source: p2
> Clause: Section I.1
> Unit: benefit_item

We shall cover Your Pet for Medically Necessary Surgery performed by Your Veterinarian for conditions covered by this Policy. We will pay for the following expenses incurred:

- Diagnostic Tests;
- Veterinarian's fee;
- operating theatre fee;
- fees and charges for anaesthesia and oxygen for them to be administered;
- surgical implants; and
- miscellaneous expenses such as prescribed drugs, injections, dressings and other medical services and Supplies related to a Surgery.

#### 2. Room and Board Expenses
> Source: p2
> Clause: Section I.2
> Unit: benefit_item

We shall cover the cost of boarding Your Pet at a licensed veterinary clinic or hospital as required by Your Veterinarian to deliver nursing care, administer medication to or monitor Your Pet. The confinement period must be no less than twelve (12) consecutive hours as a result of Surgery.

#### 3. Post Surgical Treatment Benefit
> Source: p2
> Clause: Section I.3
> Unit: benefit_item

We shall cover Your Pet for post surgical treatment, up to ninety (90) days from date of Surgery, which include follow-up consultations, diagnostic and laboratory services, examinations and investigations as required by Your Veterinarian. This benefit is also extended to cover acupuncture performed by a Veterinarian.

The post surgical treatment must have resulted directly from the Condition which the Surgery was performed and be recommended by the Veterinarian who performed the Surgery.

#### Section I Maximum Payable Limit
> Source: p2
> Clause: Section I
> Unit: coverage_rule

The maximum limit payable under Section I shall not exceed the maximum limit applicable under Clinical and Surgical Benefit.

### Section II - Chemotherapy
> Source: p2
> Clause: Section II
> Unit: benefit

We shall cover Your Pet for cost of chemotherapy incurred in a licensed veterinary clinic or hospital.

### Section III - Final Expenses Benefit
> Source: p2
> Clause: Section III
> Unit: benefit

We shall cover the fee of euthanasia and the cost of cremation, funeral service and/or handling charges from the Veterinarian or funeral service providers in respect of the handling of the remains of the Pet.

#### Section III Exclusions
> Source: p2
> Clause: Section III
> Unit: exclusion

- Transportation fee not arranged by the Veterinarian or funeral service provider;
- the cost of the niche or burial ground of the remains of the Pet.

#### Section III Co-insurance Rule
> Source: p2
> Clause: Section III
> Unit: coverage_rule

Co-insurance is not applicable to Section III.

### Overall Compensation Limit
> Source: p2
> Clause: Overall Compensation Limit
> Unit: payout

Our maximum liability under Section I to III in total shall not exceed the amount stated in the Total Annual Coverage in the Table of Benefits.

We do not reimburse the costs, fees or expenses associated with:

- Injury or Illness due to any intentional, neglectful or preventable act, including organised dog fighting, by You or a member of Your Household;

- complications of Conditions excluded or limited by this Policy;

- purchase and rental of prosthesis, corrective devices and medical appliances;

- behavioural modification, training, therapy or medications for behavioural modification;
- alternative therapies, including but not restricted to consultations and treatments involving homeopathic remedies, osteopathy, laser therapy, chiropractic treatments, stem cell therapy and/or physiotherapy, whether recommended by a Veterinarian or not;

## Limitations

### Enrolment Clinical Examination
> Source: p5
> Clause: Limitations.1
> Unit: limitation

It is mandatory that the Pet undergoes an enrolment Clinical Examination prior or within thirty (30) days from the Commencement Date of the Policy. The examination result must be produced upon the first claim. Your failure to submit Your Pet to a complete Clinical Examination may void the Policy.

### Specified Hereditary and Congenital Cover for Pets below Age Six
> Source: p5
> Clause: Limitations.2
> Unit: limitation

For Pet below six (6) years of age at the Commencement Date of the Policy, We shall cover the following specified hereditary and congenital conditions after a twelve (12) month waiting period from the Commencement Date of the Policy, provided they are not Pre-Existing Conditions:

- Elbow Dysplasia;
- Luxating Patella;
- Glaucoma;
- Cherry eye;
- Intervertebral Disk Disease (IVDD); and
- Hip Dysplasia, applicable only if it is specified and indicated in the Schedule.

Hereditary and congenital conditions not stated above are not covered.

### No Hereditary / Congenital Cover for Pets Age Six or Above
> Source: p5
> Clause: Limitations.3
> Unit: limitation

For Pet six (6) years and greater of age at the Commencement Date of the Policy, there shall be no cover for hereditary and congenital conditions.

### Spay / Neuter Related Illness Limitation
> Source: p5
> Clause: Limitations.4
> Unit: limitation

For Pet not spayed or neutered at the Commencement Date of the Policy, there shall be no cover for Illness related to prostate problems, hormonal skin conditions, perianal hernias, testicular tumours, perianal tumours, mammary tumours, uterine and ovarian conditions.

Should Your Pet be spayed or neutered after the Commencement Date of the Policy and provided no claims has been made, You can write in to Us to furnish the relevant Veterinarian report on the Spaying and Neutering procedure so that We can update Our records.
